1. As fit as a fiddle - to be in excellent physical shape or to be very healthy
2. To be back on one’s feet - you’ve successfully recovered from injury or sickness
3. A clean bill of health - when a medical professional acknowledges that you’re healthy
4. To have a spring in one’s step - means to be energetic and without worry
5. As pale as a ghost - you’re very sick or nauseous
6. To have a frog in one’s throat - you have contracted a cold or the flu, which has led to a change in your voice
7. Alive and kicking - in good health despite health problems
8. An apple a day keeps the doctor away - eating healthy foods will keep one from getting sick 
9. Break out in a cold sweat - to perspire from fever or anxiety
10. Sick as a dog - extremely ill
